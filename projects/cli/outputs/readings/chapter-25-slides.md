Study Title: The Heart of Obedience: Love, Law, and Consequence

---

Slide 1 Question: What was the cost to redeem humanity from sin, and what motivated this sacrifice?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of three crosses on a distant hill at dusk, silhouetted against a dramatic sky, symbolizing the cost of redemption.

---

Slide 2 Biblical Answer: “For God so loved the world, that He gave His only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have everlasting life.” (John 3:16)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of Jesus speaking with compassion to Nicodemus at night, soft candlelight illuminating their faces.

---

Slide 3 Basic Explanation: Before discussing obedience, we must establish the foundation of salvation. It is rooted entirely in God's sacrificial love. Redemption was not cheap; it cost the life of the Son of God.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a lamb resting near a rugged wooden cross, symbolizing innocent sacrifice.

---

Slide 4 Insight: God does not ask for our service to earn His love. Rather, He has already proved His love while we were yet sinners (Romans 5:8).

[DYK] The Greek word for "world" here is *kosmos*. It implies not just the physical planet, but the entire arranged order of humanity, often encompassing those who are hostile or indifferent to God. He loved the rebels enough to die for them.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a chaotic crowd in an ancient marketplace, with a single ray of light shining down upon them.

---

Slide 5 Question: How does Scripture define our love back to God?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a scribe writing on a parchment scroll, with an expression of deep reverence and focus.

---

Slide 6 Biblical Answer: “For this is the love of God, that we keep His commandments.” (1 John 5:3)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of the two stone tablets resting on a wooden table, bathed in a gentle, golden light.

---

Slide 7 Explanation: In the Bible, love is not merely a sentimental feeling; it is an active principle. Just as faith without works is dead, love without obedience is hollow. God defines our love for Him by our willingness to align our lives with His will.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a person kneeling in service, washing the feet of another, demonstrating active love.

---

Slide 8 Illustration: Consider a marriage. A husband may say he loves his wife, but if he ignores her requests, his words mean little. True love seeks to please the beloved. Keeping the commandments is the natural response of a heart that truly loves God.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a husband and wife in ancient garments walking hand in hand through a vineyard, looking at one another with devotion.

---

Slide 9 Question: Does God force us to obey Him?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of an open door standing alone in a landscape, inviting entry but with no one forcing the way.

---

Slide 10 Biblical Answer: “And the Spirit and the bride say, Come... And whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely.” (Revelation 22:17)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a clear stream of water flowing from a rock, with a cup resting nearby, symbolizing a free offer.

---

Slide 11 Explanation: God values free will. He does not compel obedience or force the conscience. The invitation is open to "whosoever will." He desires a relationship based on choice, not coercion.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of Jesus standing at a door and knocking, waiting for it to be opened from the inside.

---

Slide 12 Question: Why does God ask for our obedience? What is the result?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a shepherd guiding sheep toward green pastures, keeping them away from a rocky cliff edge.

---

Slide 13 Biblical Answer: “O that thou hadst hearkened to My commandments! then had thy peace been as a river, and thy righteousness as the waves of the sea.” (Isaiah 48:18)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a wide, calm river flowing gently through a lush valley at sunset.

---

Slide 14 Explanation: God’s laws are not arbitrary restrictions; they are the laws of our being, designed for our happiness. Obedience leads to "peace like a river"—constant, flowing, and life-giving. Disobedience disrupts this peace.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a peaceful ancient village scene, with families resting securely by their homes.

---

Slide 15 Definition: "Hearkened" implies more than just hearing sound; it means to listen with the intent to do and to pay close attention.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a young Samuel in the temple, listening intently with his head bowed.

---

Slide 16 Question: Does the exactness of our obedience matter to God? (The Example of Uzzah)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of the Ark of the Covenant being transported on a cart, with oxen pulling it along a dusty road.

---

Slide 17 Biblical Answer: “And when they came to Nachon's threshing floor, Uzzah put forth his hand to the ark of God and took hold of it... And the anger of the Lord was kindled against Uzzah; and God smote him there for his error...” (2 Samuel 6:6-7)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a man reaching out to steady the Ark, with a sudden, dramatic shift in the lighting indicating a divine intervention.

---

Slide 18 Explanation: This seems severe, but God had given specific instructions that the Ark was only to be carried on the shoulders of the Levites and never touched. David and the people ignored the command and used a "new cart" instead. Uzzah’s death underscores that good intentions do not replace obedience to God's explicit Word.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of Levite priests properly carrying the Ark on poles upon their shoulders, walking with solemnity.

---

Slide 19 Speaker Note: The "New Cart"

[SN] The Philistines used a cart to move the Ark earlier in history. When David used a cart, he was borrowing a method from the pagans rather than following God's sanctified method. We cannot use worldly methods to do God's holy work and expect His blessing.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting contrasting a rough wooden cart with the golden, holy poles of the sanctuary.

---

Slide 20 Question: What warning does Jesus give regarding looking back at the world?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a figure walking forward but turning their head completely around to look at something behind them.

---

Slide 21 Biblical Answer: “Remember Lot's wife.” (Luke 17:32)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a lone pillar of white salt standing in a desolate landscape, with the smoke of a destroyed city in the far distance.

---

Slide 22 Explanation: Lot’s wife was physically moving away from Sodom, but her heart remained there. When she looked back, she disobeyed the specific command and was turned into a pillar of salt. We cannot be saved while holding on to the sins God has called us to leave behind.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a family fleeing a city gate, with one woman lagging behind, her expression full of longing for the city.

---

Slide 23 Illustration: A farmer plowing a field cannot make a straight furrow if he is constantly looking over his shoulder. To move toward the Kingdom, our eyes must be fixed forward on Christ.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a farmer guiding a plow behind an ox, staring straight ahead at a fixed point in the distance.

---

Slide 24 Question: Can human tradition replace God’s commandments in worship?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a group of elders in robes debating intensely, holding large scrolls.

---

Slide 25 Biblical Answer: “But in vain they do worship Me, teaching for doctrines the commandments of men.” (Matthew 15:9)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of Jesus speaking firmly to the Pharisees, gesturing towards the Scriptures.

---

Slide 26 Explanation: Jesus clearly states that worship based on human tradition, which sets aside God’s explicit law, is "vain" or empty. Our religion must be based on what God has actually said, not on cultural customs.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a single candle burning brightly next to an open Bible, casting light on the text.

---

Slide 27 Deeper Insight: Throughout history, the conflict has often been between the Word of God and the traditions of men. From Eden to the end times, the test of loyalty is whether we will obey God or follow our own reasoning.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of the tree of knowledge of good and evil, with a serpent coiled in the branches, contrasted with a simple altar of worship.

---

Slide 28 Practical Application

• Are there areas in my life where I am following "tradition" rather than Scripture?
• Do I view God's commandments as a burden, or as the path to peace?
• Am I carrying the presence of God in the way He asked, or am I using a "new cart" of my own making?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a person kneeling in a quiet room, head bowed in deep reflection and prayer.

---

Slide 29 Conclusion: God calls us to obedience not to burden us, but because He loves us and knows that sin destroys.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a shepherd carrying a lamb on his shoulders, walking into a golden sunrise.
