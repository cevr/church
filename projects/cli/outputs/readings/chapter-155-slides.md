Study Title: Returning to the Source: Understanding and Healing Backsliding

---

Slide 1 Question: What is the nature of backsliding, and what are its results?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a figure standing at a fork in a dirt road, looking back toward a path of light while standing in shadow.

---

Slide 2 Biblical Answer: “Thine own wickedness shall correct thee, and thy backslidings shall reprove thee; know therefore and see that it is an evil thing and bitter, that thou hast forsaken the Lord thy God...” (Jeremiah 2:19)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a desolate landscape with a lone figure sitting in sorrow, soft light highlighting their regret.

---

Slide 3 Explanation: Backsliding is not merely breaking a rule; it is an abandonment of the source of life. The text describes it as "evil and bitter."

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a withered branch broken off from a lush, glowing olive tree.

---

Slide 4 Insight: Interestingly, the punishment for backsliding is inherent in the act itself—separation from God naturally results in spiritual correction and sorrow.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a man walking into a dark wilderness, the light of a city fading far behind him.

---

Slide 5 Did You Know? The term "backsliding" comes from an agricultural metaphor of a heifer or ox sliding backward or refusing to pull the yoke. It implies a stubborn resistance to moving forward with God.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a stubborn ox planting its feet and refusing to move forward in a field, soft golden hour light.

---

Slide 6 Question: Is there a natural tendency in the human heart to drift from God?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a crowd of people wandering aimlessly in a desert, distracted and looking in various directions.

---

Slide 7 Biblical Answer: “And My people are bent to backsliding from Me.” (Hosea 11:7)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a bent reed or a crooked tree growing away from the sunlight.

---

Slide 8 Explanation: The prophet Hosea notes that humanity is "bent" or inclined toward wandering. Jeremiah adds that this happens when no man asks, "What have I done?" Without self-reflection, people rush into their own courses.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a man staring into a polished bronze mirror, contemplating his reflection in dim light.

---

Slide 9 Illustration: Imagine a warhorse charging blindly into the chaos of battle, unaware of the danger. Similarly, when we fail to pause and examine our spiritual condition, we often rush headlong into spiritual danger.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a powerful warhorse galloping fiercely into dust and battle smoke, eyes wide and focused only on speed.

---

Slide 10 Question: How does God view our departure from Him?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a saddened king looking out from a balcony, watching a loved one leave in the distance.

---

Slide 11 Biblical Answer: “Surely as a wife treacherously departeth from her husband, so have ye dealt treacherously with Me, O house of Israel, saith the Lord.” (Jeremiah 3:20)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a tearful separation, symbolizing a broken marriage covenant, soft emotional lighting.

---

Slide 12 Explanation: God uses the most intimate human relationship—marriage—to describe His bond with us. Backsliding is not just a legal violation; it is a personal heartbreak to God, viewed as treachery against a covenant of love.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of two hands letting go of each other, with a wedding ring visible, soft and somber tones.

---

Slide 13 Question: What is the remedy for backsliding?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a steep, rocky path leading upward toward a bright, breaking dawn.

---

Slide 14 Biblical Answer: “Let the wicked forsake his way, and the unrighteous man his thoughts; and let him return unto the Lord, and He will have mercy upon him... for He will abundantly pardon.” (Isaiah 55:7)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a father running with open arms to embrace a returning figure in rags.

---

Slide 15 Step-by-Step Restoration:
1. **Acknowledge:** Admit the transgression.
2. **Forsake:** Turn away from the wrong path and thoughts.
3. **Return:** Come back to the Lord, who waits with mercy.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a man kneeling in prayer on a stone floor, a shaft of divine light illuminating his bowed head.

---

Slide 16 Speaker Note: Isaiah calls for forsaking "thoughts," not just actions. True revival begins in the mind—aligning our thinking with God’s perspective rather than our own carnal reasoning.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a contemplative man with a peaceful expression, light radiating near his head to symbolize renewed mindedness.

---

Slide 17 Question: How can a believer be secured against sliding back?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a strong stone fortress standing firm against crashing ocean waves.

---

Slide 18 Biblical Answer: “The law of his God is in his heart; none of his steps shall slide.” (Psalm 37:31)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a traveler walking confidently on slippery rocks, finding sure footing.

---

Slide 19 Explanation: Stability comes from internalization. When the Law of God moves from stone tablets or external rules into the heart (affections and mind), obedience becomes natural, and one’s spiritual footing becomes sure.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a heart glowing with internal golden light, surrounded by shadow.

---

Slide 20 Question: What specific practical test does God sometimes use to call His people back?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a set of ancient scales and weights on a wooden table.

---

Slide 21 Biblical Answer: “Return unto Me, and I will return unto you... But ye said, Wherein shall we return? ... In tithes and offerings.” (Malachi 3:7-8)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of hands presenting a basket of grain and fruits at the temple altar.

---

Slide 22 Explanation: When the heart slides from God, stewardship often declines. God invites His people to "prove" Him by restoring faithfulness in tithes and offerings, promising a blessing too large to receive.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of the windows of heaven opening, pouring down golden light and rain upon a field.

---

Slide 23 Question: Is there hope for the backslider?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a single ray of sunlight breaking through a dark, stormy sky.

---

Slide 24 Biblical Answer: “I will heal their backsliding, I will love them freely: for Mine anger is turned away from him.” (Hosea 14:4)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a shepherd gently tending to the wounded leg of a sheep.

---

Slide 25 Explanation: God views backsliding as a disease that needs healing rather than a crime requiring only execution. He offers to "heal" the wandering heart and love the repentant sinner freely.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of Jesus reaching out to touch and heal a sick person, soft compassionate lighting.

---

Slide 26 Spirit-Led Tangent: The concept of "healing" backsliding suggests that sin damages the soul like a physical wound. Just as Jesus healed the body, His High Priestly ministry heals the character of those who return to Him.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of the High Priest in the Holy Place, incense rising, representing intercession and healing.

---

Slide 27 Question: Why is vigilance especially necessary in the last days?

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a watchman standing on a city wall at twilight, looking out over a darkening horizon.

---

Slide 28 Biblical Answer: “And because iniquity shall abound, the love of many shall wax cold.” (Matthew 24:12)

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a cold, winter scene in an ancient city, people walking past each other without looking, muted colors.

---

Slide 29 Explanation: As the world becomes more lawless (iniquity abounding), the spiritual atmosphere chills. The danger for the Christian is that their love for God and truth will grow cold.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a dying campfire with only a few glowing embers left in the dark.

---

Slide 30 Practical Application: To prevent this cooling of love, Jesus commands us to "Watch and pray" and to "Examine yourselves." Daily communion keeps the fire of love burning.

[IMG] Warm classical biblical painting of a person adding fresh wood to a fire, causing the flames to rise bright and warm.
